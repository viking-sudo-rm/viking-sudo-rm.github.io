About

I am interested in the intersection of language and computationâ€”spanning NLP, linguistics, and formal language theory. In particular, I enjoy doing formal work investigating how neural networks can learn to represent the structure and meaning of natural languages.

I just started as a first-year Ph.D. student at the CDS at NYU, where I am supported by an NSF GSRF. I am thankful to have worked with Noah Smith, Yoav Goldberg, and Roy Schwartz as a PYI on the AllenNLP team at AI2. As an undergrad at Yale, I was a member of CLAY and wrote a thesis with Bob Frank and Dana Angluin, both of whom were inspiring mentors.
